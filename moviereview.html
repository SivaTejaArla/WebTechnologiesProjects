BABY

9/10

Synopsis

Baby is a film that has earned a good buzz due to its chartbuster songs. Directed by Sai Rajesh, the film stars Anand Deverakonda, Vaishnavi Chaitanya, and Viraj Ashwin in the lead roles. The movie hit the screens today, and let’s see how it is.

Plot

Vaishnavi (Vaishnavi Chaitanya) and Anand (Anand Deverakonda), hailing from a slum, are high school lovebirds. Anand fails to make it to the college and becomes an auto driver, whereas Vaishnavi joins the college. Vaishnavi undergoes a massive transformation, and this is also the time she gets close to Viraj (Viraj Ashwin). Slowly problems start developing between Vaishnavi and Anand. An unexpected incident takes place, which changes the lives of Viraj, Anand, and Vaishnavi as a whole. What happened next? How did the incident affect the three? This forms part of the crux of the story.

Artists Review:

Anand Devarakonda lived in the role of a young auto driver. He performed the role well as an innocent and sincere lover who goes to any extent to keep his lady love happy. He looked innocent while expressing his love and showed good emotional outbursts when he comes to know about the dark secrets of his lover. His body language, dialogue delivery, and expressions are natural and realistic. However at times, seeing him on screen, one gets a feeling that he is imitating his brother Vijay Devarakonda while trying to show more powerful emotions and intensity. He showed good feelings as a protective and possessive lover. Vaishnavi Chaitanya steals the hearts of all with her intense and impactful performance. Vaishnavi Chaitanya's expressions and emotions while showing variations in her character are top-class. She did full justice to her role and carried the film on her shoulders as the entire film revolved around her. She not only delivered bold and powerful dialogues but also looked ultra glamorous. She also did intimate and kissing scenes with ease. Her scenes with Anand Devarakonda and Viraj Ashwin came out pretty well. Viraj Ashwin looked stylish and handsome in the role of a rich and affluent student. He came up with good performance and at times dominated the lead pair. He also elevated the scenes with his expressions and emotions. The scenes featuring him, Anand Devarakonda, and Vaishnavi Chaitanya are good.

Technicians Review:

Baby story readied by Sai Rajesh is a youthful romantic entertainer with an interesting twist. Sai Rajesh seems to have drawn inspiration from the real-life happenings in the Genex love stories and came up with the hard-hitting love story. He started the narration in a simple manner showing the blossoming of love between the lead pair Anand and Vaishnavi. Then Viraj is introduced and as the story progresses in a routine predictable and slow pace, the first half assumes the routine shades. However, the way the scenes between Aand and Vaishhavi are shown on the screen, maintained the curiosity feel among movie lovers intact. The introduction of Viraj and the happenings between Viraj and Vaishnavi liven up the proceedings. The first half ends with a decent interval twist setting the tone for the second half. The real story starts in the second half and emotions reach the next level as the story progresses. The powerful dialogues and screenplay and script keep viewers glued to the screen. Sai Rajesh got the optimum performances from Anand, Viraj, and Vaishnavi and this elevated the emotions taking the film to another level.
